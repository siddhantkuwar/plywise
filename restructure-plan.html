02 Review as the product

Put the board, the move, and the evidence in one calm frame.

Replace sloppy pieces, imprecise arrows, crowded rails, and ambiguous loading with a board-first workspace and observable engine telemetry.

  • Use a deliberate 2D SVG Staunton set, real square coordinates, and pixel-accurate overlay geometry.
  • Replace Train with Explore in navigation; keep Review uncluttered.
  • Show parser, shallow, and deep analysis as real stages with skeletons and live status.
  • Expose Stockfish depth, nodes/s, MultiPV, elapsed time, and failure/retry state.
Design rule

Transparent work, not fake reasoning.

The engine visual can feel alive with a waveform, pulse, and staged transitions. It must never invent chain-of-thought or pretend that analysis is ongoing after it has finished.

A 250–450 ms minimum transition prevents flicker. It is a UI rhythm, not a fake loading delay.

03 Explore and visual progress

Make the next step feel like a chess space, not a SaaS dashboard.

Explore gives the app a useful learning library. Progress tells a small, sourced story about rating, games, and analysis over time.

  • Ship a focused local library: Openings, Middlegames, Endgames.
  • Link concepts back to a user's own positions and games.
  • Show current Rapid rating, 30-day delta, and a clean rating line from local sync snapshots.
  • Use rings only for named, explainable activity—not an imaginary chess-ability score.
Scope discipline

Start with a usable library, not an encyclopedia.

Seed 12–20 high-value concepts. Every entry carries a source, version, FEN, key plan, model moves, and linked personal examples.

No opaque score.

Rating → dated public snapshot
Activity → local game/event count
Concepts → source-attributed content

The old drill machinery can stay in the backend until a better training simulation earns its place.
